How rebalancing works
Rebalancing restores your intended risk profile by selling overweight assets and buying underweight ones. It enforces discipline by locking in gains from outperformers and deploying into laggards at lower prices. Aim to rebalance when any class drifts more than ±5% from target, or at least once per year. Note: selling in India attracts STCG (20%) or LTCG (12.5%) — factor this in before executing. Consider rebalancing via new SIP deployment into underweight assets first, to minimise taxable events.
